## Scope summary

Subpart M (1926.500–503) is the most-cited OSHA standard in construction. 1926.501 establishes the **duty** to provide fall protection; 1926.502 defines the **criteria** for each protection system; 1926.503 covers **training**. The general rule is that fall protection is required at heights of 6 feet or more above a lower level. Specific activities (residential construction, leading edges, roofing, scaffolds) have tailored provisions. Scaffolding fall protection is covered by 1926.502(d) but the scaffold standard (1926.451) is the primary reference.

## 1926.501(b)(1) — Unprotected Sides and Edges (≥ 6 feet)

### Source excerpt

> *Each employee on a walking/working surface (horizontal and vertical surface) with an unprotected side or edge which is 6 feet (1.8 m) or more above a lower level shall be protected from falling by the use of guardrail systems, safety net systems, or personal fall arrest systems.*

## 1926.503 — Training

### Source excerpt

> *The employer shall provide a training program for each employee who might be exposed to fall hazards. The program shall enable each employee to recognize the hazards of falling and shall train each employee in the procedures to be followed in order to minimize these hazards.*

> *The employer shall assure that each employee has been trained ... by a competent person qualified in the following areas: (1) The nature of fall hazards in the work area; (2) The correct procedures for erecting, maintaining, disassembling, and inspecting the fall protection systems to be used; (3) The use and operation of guardrail systems, personal fall arrest systems, safety net systems ...; (4) The role of each employee in the safety monitoring system when this system is used; (5) The limitations on the use of mechanical equipment during the performance of roofing work ...*

### `tests/1926.501/test_b1_unprotected_edges.py`

```python
"""
1926.501(b)(1) — Unprotected Sides and Edges
Confidence: HIGH | Human Review: NOT REQUIRED
"""

FALL_PROTECTION_HEIGHT_FT = 6
ACCEPTED_PROTECTION_METHODS = {"guardrail", "safety_net", "PFAS"}


def test_fall_protection_installed_at_all_unprotected_edges(fall_protection_records):
    violations = []
    for record in fall_protection_records:
        if record.get("height_feet", 0) < FALL_PROTECTION_HEIGHT_FT:
            continue
        if not record.get("protection_installed"):
            violations.append(
                f"Location {record['location_id']}: {record['height_feet']}ft "
                f"unprotected edge with no fall protection"
            )
        elif record.get("protection_method") not in ACCEPTED_PROTECTION_METHODS:
            violations.append(
                f"Location {record['location_id']}: protection method "
                f"'{record['protection_method']}' is not an accepted system"
            )
    assert not violations, (
        f"CRITICAL VIOLATION: {len(violations)} location(s) without adequate "
        f"fall protection at ≥{FALL_PROTECTION_HEIGHT_FT} feet:\n"
        + "\n".join(violations)
    )


def test_holes_covered_or_guarded(hole_inventory):
    violations = []
    for hole in hole_inventory:
        if not hole.get("protection_type"):
            violations.append(
                f"Hole {hole['location_id']}: no protection (cover or guardrail) in place"
            )
        elif hole.get("protection_type") == "cover":
            if not hole.get("cover_load_rated"):
                violations.append(
                    f"Hole {hole['location_id']}: cover present but not load-rated "
                    f"(must support 2x max intended load)"
                )
    assert not violations, (
        f"CRITICAL VIOLATION: {len(violations)} hole(s) without adequate protection:\n"
        + "\n".join(violations)
    )
```

             if i["equipment_id"] == assignment["equipment_id"]

                f"{assignment['use_date']}: no pre-use inspection recorded"
            )

        f"VIOLATION: {len(violations)} PFAS equipment item(s) used without "
        f"pre-use inspection:\n" + "\n".join(violations)

## Notes for the registry

- **6-foot trigger vs. other heights:** Some OSHA standards use different height thresholds (e.g., excavations require fall protection at 6 feet depth for workers near the edge; scaffolds: 10 feet for construction). Always confirm the applicable standard's threshold — do not assume 6 feet is universal.
- **Controlled access zones (CAZ):** Permitted for certain leading-edge, precast concrete, and residential construction activities. The CAZ perimeter must be set at specific distances from the edge and must be monitored. CAZ is NOT a substitute for conventional fall protection on roofs unless a safety monitoring system is present.
- **Warning line systems:** Permitted for low-slope roofing when combined with safety monitoring or other protection. The warning line must be at least 6 feet from the roof edge (or 10 feet for specific machinery). Lines must be rigged/installed per specific specifications in 1926.502(f).
- **Multi-employer sites:** The fall protection duty falls on the employer whose workers are exposed. The GC may be cited as the controlling employer even if a subcontractor's workers are exposed.
