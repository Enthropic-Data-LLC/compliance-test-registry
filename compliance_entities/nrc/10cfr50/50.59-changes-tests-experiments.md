# NRC 10 CFR §50.59 — Changes, Tests, and Experiments

**Spec file:** `50.59-changes-tests-experiments.md`
**Registry version:** 2026.05
**Last updated:** 2026-05-21
**Parent index:** [`_index.md`](./_index.md)
**Regulatory basis:** 10 CFR §50.59; NEI 96-07 Rev. 1 (NRC-endorsed screening/evaluation guidance); NUREG-1866 (§50.59 staff guidance)
**Authority:** U.S. Nuclear Regulatory Commission
**Enforcing context:** All 10 CFR Part 50 / Part 52 operating licensees; applies to changes to the facility, procedures, or tests/experiments

---

## Overview

§50.59 allows nuclear power plant licensees to make changes to the facility, procedures, or tests and experiments **without prior NRC approval** — provided the change does not require a license amendment. The regulation establishes a 10-criterion evaluation process to determine whether a change creates an "unreviewed safety question" (USQ) that mandates NRC review before implementation.

**This section is the most CONTESTED in routine nuclear plant operations.** A negative §50.59 evaluation outcome (change requires license amendment) carries significant cost and schedule impact. Evaluations require licensed professional judgment.

### The two-step process

```
Any proposed change to facility, procedures, or tests/experiments
    │
    ▼
Step 1: §50.59 SCREENING
    ├── Is a change being made? (Y/N)
    ├── Is an associated activity being performed? (T&E)
    └── Does any §50.59(c)(2) threshold criterion apply?
          │
          NO criterion applies → No §50.59 evaluation needed (document basis)
          │
          YES criterion may apply → §50.59 EVALUATION required
                    │
                    ▼
Step 2: §50.59 EVALUATION (10 criteria per §50.59(c)(2))
          │
          ALL 10 criteria = NO → Change can proceed without NRC approval
          │
          ANY criterion = YES → License amendment required BEFORE implementation
```

### The 10 evaluation criteria (§50.59(c)(2))

A change requires a license amendment if it would:

| # | Criterion | Confidence |
|---|-----------|-----------|
| 1 | Result in more than a minimal increase in the frequency of occurrence of an accident previously evaluated in the FSAR | CONTESTED — probabilistic judgment |
| 2 | Result in more than a minimal increase in the likelihood of occurrence of a malfunction of an SSC important to safety previously evaluated in the FSAR | CONTESTED — probabilistic judgment |
| 3 | Result in more than a minimal increase in the consequences of an accident previously evaluated in the FSAR | PARAMETERIZED — dose calculation thresholds exist |
| 4 | Result in more than a minimal increase in the consequences of a malfunction of an SSC important to safety previously evaluated in the FSAR | PARAMETERIZED — dose calculation thresholds |
| 5 | Create a possibility for an accident of a different type than any previously evaluated in the FSAR | CONTESTED — "different type" determination |
| 6 | Create a possibility for a malfunction of an SSC important to safety with a different result than any previously evaluated in the FSAR | CONTESTED — "different result" determination |
| 7 | Result in a design basis limit for a fission product barrier (fuel cladding, RCS pressure boundary, primary containment) being exceeded or altered | PARAMETERIZED — explicit design limits |
| 8 | Result in a departure from a method of evaluation described in the FSAR used in establishing design bases or in the safety analyses | CONTESTED — method departure determination |
| 9 | Conflict with technical specifications | DETERMINISTIC — direct TS comparison |
| 10 | Conflict with administrative controls described in the license (Certificate of Compliance for Part 52) | DETERMINISTIC — direct license comparison |

### Tests — screening program completeness

```python
import pytest


class TestFiftyFiftyNineScreening:
    """Pattern 2: PARAMETERIZED — screening scope determination requires engineering judgment."""

    @pytest.mark.assumption(
        id="ASSUME-NRC50-5059-001",
        description=(
            "§50.59 screening scope: §50.59 applies to any change to the facility or "
            "procedures described in the FSAR, and to tests/experiments not described "
            "in the FSAR. The threshold is LOWER than 'safety-related' — it applies to "
            "non-safety-related SSCs that are described in the FSAR and may affect "
            "FSAR-described analyses. The Updated FSAR (UFSAR) is the reference document; "
            "a change that is not described in the UFSAR does not require §50.59 screening. "
            "SCREENING is NOT the same as EVALUATION — screening determines whether an "
            "evaluation is needed; a negative screening (no evaluation needed) is acceptable "
            "with a documented basis."
        ),
        approved_by="50.59 Coordinator / Licensing",
        review_date="2026-05-21",
    )
    def test_change_package_has_50_59_screening_documented(self, change_record: dict):
        """Every change to a FSAR-described activity must have a §50.59 screening on record."""
        is_fsar_described = change_record.get("affects_fsar_described_activity", None)
        screening_document = change_record.get("fifty_fifty_nine_screening_document_number")

        if is_fsar_described is False:
            # Explicitly determined not FSAR-described — no §50.59 screening needed
            basis = change_record.get("not_fsar_described_basis")
            assert basis is not None, (
                f"Change '{change_record.get('change_number')}': marked as not affecting "
                "FSAR-described activity but no documented basis — the determination that "
                "§50.59 does not apply must itself be documented"
            )
            return

        assert screening_document is not None, (
            f"Change '{change_record.get('change_number')}': no §50.59 screening on record — "
            "all changes to FSAR-described facilities, procedures, or tests/experiments "
            "require a §50.59 screening before implementation per §50.59(d)(1)"
        )

    def test_screening_conducted_before_implementation(self, change_record: dict):
        screening_date = change_record.get("fifty_fifty_nine_screening_date")
        implementation_date = change_record.get("implementation_date")

        if screening_date is None or implementation_date is None:
            pytest.skip("Screening or implementation date not recorded")

        assert screening_date <= implementation_date, (
            f"Change '{change_record.get('change_number')}': §50.59 screening completed "
            f"on {screening_date} AFTER implementation on {implementation_date} — "
            "§50.59 screening/evaluation must be performed and documented BEFORE "
            "implementing any change; post-implementation screening is a §50.59 violation"
        )

    def test_screening_identifies_applicable_fsar_sections(self, change_record: dict):
        screening_doc = change_record.get("fifty_fifty_nine_screening_document_number")
        if screening_doc is None:
            pytest.skip("No screening document — handled by earlier test")

        fsar_sections_cited = change_record.get("fsar_sections_cited_in_screening", [])
        assert len(fsar_sections_cited) > 0, (
            f"Change '{change_record.get('change_number')}': §50.59 screening document "
            f"{screening_doc} cites no FSAR sections — screening must identify the specific "
            "UFSAR section(s) that describe the facility/procedures affected by this change; "
            "absence of FSAR citations indicates an incomplete screening"
        )
```

## Section 3 — Evaluation Completion and Approval Process (DETERMINISTIC)

### Requirements extracted

**Source:** 10 CFR §50.59(d); NEI 96-07 Rev. 1 §5.0

| # | Subject | Condition | Obligation | Evidence |
|---|---------|-----------|------------|---------|
| 3.1 | Written evaluation before implementation | If a §50.59 evaluation is required (any §50.59(c)(2) criterion potentially applies) | §50.59 evaluation shall be completed and documented BEFORE implementation; retroactive evaluations are violations of §50.59(d)(1) | Evaluation document; implementation date comparison |

| 3.4 | Summary of changes submitted to NRC | With periodic FSAR update | A summary of all changes, tests, and experiments performed under §50.59 since last FSAR update submitted to NRC with updated FSAR | NRC submittal; §50.59 log |

### DETERMINISTIC thresholds

| Obligation | Threshold | Source |
|---|---|---|
| §50.59 evaluation — before implementation | BEFORE implementation; no retroactive evaluations | §50.59(d)(1) |
| FSAR update — incorporate §50.59 changes | Within 24 months of change implementation | §50.59(d)(2) (or 50.71(e) for UFSAR) |
| Any "Yes" criterion result | NRC license amendment required BEFORE implementation | §50.59(c)(2) |

```python
import pytest
from datetime import date
from dateutil.relativedelta import relativedelta

FSAR_UPDATE_MAX_INTERVAL_MONTHS = 24  # 2-year maximum UFSAR update cycle


class TestFiftyFiftyNineProcess:
    """Pattern 1/2: evaluation timing is DETERMINISTIC; qualification is PARAMETERIZED."""

    @pytest.mark.assumption(
        id="ASSUME-NRC50-5059-004",
        description=(
            "§50.59 evaluation timing and FSAR update: evaluations must be completed BEFORE "
            "implementation — there is no provision for retroactive §50.59 evaluations in the "
            "regulation (though NRC allows corrective action when discovered). FSAR updates "
            "incorporating all §50.59 changes must be submitted to NRC within the periodic "
            "update cycle — maximum 24 months per 10 CFR 50.71(e). A summary list of all "
            "§50.59 changes is submitted with the FSAR update. Failure to update the FSAR "
            "within 24 months is a separate violation of 50.71(e) — distinct from the §50.59 "
            "process violation of implementing without a completed evaluation."
        ),
        approved_by="50.59 Coordinator / Licensing",
        review_date="2026-05-21",
    )
    def test_all_evaluation_criteria_documented_with_yes_or_no(self, evaluation_record: dict):
        """Completeness check: every evaluation must document all 10 criteria."""
        evaluation_number = evaluation_record.get("evaluation_number")
        criteria_results = {
            f"criterion_{i}_result": evaluation_record.get(f"criterion_{i}_result")
            for i in range(1, 11)
        }

        missing_criteria = [k for k, v in criteria_results.items() if v is None]
        assert not missing_criteria, (
            f"Evaluation '{evaluation_number}': criteria not documented: "
            f"{[c.replace('criterion_', 'Criterion ').replace('_result', '') for c in missing_criteria]} — "
            "all 10 §50.59(c)(2) criteria must be explicitly evaluated; an incomplete "
            "evaluation does not satisfy §50.59(d)(1) requirements"
        )

    def test_any_yes_criterion_has_license_amendment_request(self, evaluation_record: dict):
        evaluation_number = evaluation_record.get("evaluation_number")
        yes_criteria = [
            i for i in range(1, 11)
            if evaluation_record.get(f"criterion_{i}_result") == "Yes"
        ]

        if not yes_criteria:
            return  # All No / Not Applicable — change can proceed without amendment

        license_amendment = evaluation_record.get("license_amendment_request_number")
        assert license_amendment is not None, (
            f"Evaluation '{evaluation_number}': criteria {yes_criteria} returned 'Yes' — "
            "one or more criteria indicate this change requires NRC prior approval — but no "
            "license amendment request number is documented; 10 CFR §50.59(c)(2) prohibits "
            "implementing any change that requires a license amendment without prior NRC approval"
        )

    def test_evaluation_completed_before_implementation(self, evaluation_record: dict):
        evaluation_date = evaluation_record.get("evaluation_completion_date")
        implementation_date = evaluation_record.get("implementation_date")

        if evaluation_date is None or implementation_date is None:
            pytest.skip("Evaluation or implementation date not recorded")

        assert evaluation_date <= implementation_date, (
            f"Evaluation '{evaluation_record.get('evaluation_number')}': evaluation "
            f"completed {evaluation_date} AFTER implementation on {implementation_date} — "
            "§50.59(d)(1) requires written evaluation BEFORE implementation; retroactive "
            "evaluations are violations regardless of the final evaluation outcome"
        )

    def test_fsar_update_not_overdue_for_50_59_changes(self, plant_50_59_log: dict):
        last_fsar_update = plant_50_59_log.get("last_fsar_update_submitted_to_nrc_date")
        assert last_fsar_update is not None, (
            "No FSAR update on record — 10 CFR 50.71(e) requires that the UFSAR be updated "
            "and submitted to NRC within 24 months of any §50.59 change implemented since "
            "the last FSAR update"
        )

        due_date = last_fsar_update + relativedelta(months=FSAR_UPDATE_MAX_INTERVAL_MONTHS)
        today = date.today()
        assert today <= due_date, (
            f"FSAR/UFSAR update to NRC overdue — last update: {last_fsar_update}, "
            f"24-month deadline: {due_date}, today: {today} — "
            "10 CFR 50.71(e) requires UFSAR updates with a summary of §50.59 changes "
            "within 24 months of the changes being implemented"
        )

    def test_50_59_change_log_includes_all_implementations(self, plant_50_59_log: dict):
        logged_change_count = plant_50_59_log.get("total_changes_logged_since_last_fsar_update", 0)
        change_control_count = plant_50_59_log.get("implemented_changes_since_last_fsar_update", 0)

        # Implemented changes with completed §50.59 screening/evaluation should equal
        # what's in the log — a discrepancy suggests untracked changes
        assert logged_change_count >= change_control_count * 0.95, (
            f"§50.59 change log has {logged_change_count} entries but change control system "
            f"shows {change_control_count} implemented FSAR-applicable changes since last "
            "FSAR update — discrepancy suggests §50.59 screenings not being generated for "
            "all changes; this is a programmatic §50.59 implementation gap"
        )
```

```python
import pytest


class TestsAndExperiments:
    """Pattern 2: PARAMETERIZED — T&E scope determination requires engineering judgment."""

    @pytest.mark.assumption(
        id="ASSUME-NRC50-5059-005",
        description=(
            "§50.59 tests and experiments: a test or experiment that is described in the "
            "current FSAR does not require §50.59 screening for that description. If the "
            "test involves configurations, conditions, or methods not described in the FSAR, "
            "a §50.59 screening is required. Reactor physics tests (startup tests), "
            "special maintenance tests, and surveillance testing under new conditions are "
            "common T&E areas. Temporary modifications required to conduct a test are "
            "themselves §50.59 changes — they receive separate §50.59 treatment."
        ),
        approved_by="50.59 Coordinator",
        review_date="2026-05-21",
    )
    def test_test_or_experiment_has_50_59_screening(self, test_authorization: dict):
        is_fsar_described = test_authorization.get("test_described_in_fsar", None)

        if is_fsar_described:
            return  # FSAR-described tests do not require §50.59 screening

        screening_document = test_authorization.get("fifty_fifty_nine_screening_document_number")
        assert screening_document is not None, (
            f"Test/experiment '{test_authorization.get('test_authorization_number')}': "
            "not described in FSAR but no §50.59 screening on record — §50.59(a)(2) requires "
            "screening of tests or experiments not described in the FSAR before conducting them"
        )

    def test_temporary_modifications_for_test_have_50_59_screening(self, test_authorization: dict):
        temp_mods = test_authorization.get("temporary_modifications", [])

        for temp_mod in temp_mods:
            mod_id = temp_mod.get("temporary_mod_id")
            screening_document = temp_mod.get("fifty_fifty_nine_screening_document_number")
            assert screening_document is not None, (
                f"Temporary modification '{mod_id}' (associated with test "
                f"'{test_authorization.get('test_authorization_number')}'): no §50.59 "
                "screening on record — temporary modifications are §50.59 changes; each "
                "requires screening even when made solely to conduct a test"
            )

    def test_temporary_modifications_restored_after_test(self, test_authorization: dict):
        if not test_authorization.get("test_complete", False):
            pytest.skip("Test not yet complete")

        temp_mods = test_authorization.get("temporary_modifications", [])
        for temp_mod in temp_mods:
            mod_id = temp_mod.get("temporary_mod_id")
            restored = temp_mod.get("restored_after_test", False)
            assert restored, (
                f"Temporary modification '{mod_id}' (test "
                f"'{test_authorization.get('test_authorization_number')}'): not verified "
                "as restored after test completion — all temporary configurations must be "
                "removed and verified restored per the test procedure before return to normal operation"
            )
```

## Contested items

| Item | Reason | Resolution path |
|---|---|---|
| "More than minimal" — Criteria 1 & 2 | No quantitative threshold; NRC enforcement history shows frequent disagreement between licensee and NRC staff evaluators on what constitutes "more than minimal" | Licensed nuclear engineer evaluation per NEI 96-07 §4.3.1–4.3.2; for borderline cases, informal NRC staff consultation or written request for NRC review |
| "Different type" / "different result" — Criteria 5 & 6 | Common misapplication: evaluators focus on whether existing accidents are worse rather than asking whether any new initiating event or failure mode is created | NEI 96-07 §4.3.5–4.3.6 decision tree; SRO or nuclear engineer with detailed FSAR accident analysis knowledge |
| "Departure from evaluation method" — Criterion 8 | Most litigated criterion; distinction between using an approved method (no departure) and departing from it (amendment needed) has no bright-line rule | Licensing staff with NRC methodology approval documentation; NRC enforcement orders for prior violations provide guidance on where the line falls |
| §50.59 applicability to non-safety SSCs | Whether §50.59 applies to modifications of non-safety-related SSCs that are described in the FSAR requires FSAR scope determination | Engineering review of FSAR section cross-reference; §50.59 screening document citing specific FSAR sections |
