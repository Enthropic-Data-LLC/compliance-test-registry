# FDA 21 CFR Parts 210 & 211 — Current Good Manufacturing Practice (cGMP) for Finished Pharmaceuticals

**Registry version:** 2026.05
**Last updated:** 2026-05-20
**Scope:** 21 CFR Part 210 (definitions and scope) + 21 CFR Part 211 (cGMP requirements for finished drug products)
**Authority:** U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA) / CDER, CBER
**Enforcing context:** All manufacturers, processors, packers, and holders of finished pharmaceutical products (prescription, OTC, investigational drugs, biologics covered under 21 CFR Parts 600–680) offered for sale in the U.S.; applies to domestic facilities and foreign facilities exporting to U.S.
**Note:** This is the primary U.S. pharmaceutical cGMP regulation. Companion standards: ICH Q7 (API manufacturing), ICH Q10 (pharmaceutical quality system), EU GMP Annex series, WHO GMP guidelines. 21 CFR Part 11 (electronic records) applies to any computer systems used in GMP activities.

## Key retention periods (DETERMINISTIC)

| Record | Minimum retention |
|---|---|
| Batch records | 1 year after expiration date OR 3 years after batch distribution (whichever is longer) |
| Lab records | Same as batch records |
| Distribution records | Same as batch records |
| Complaint files | 1 year after expiration date OR 3 years after distribution |
| Reserve samples | 1 year after expiration (at least twice the quantity for all required testing) |

## Cross-standard dependencies

| Shared artifact | Frameworks | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Batch records | 21 CFR Part 211 §211.188, EU GMP Annex 11, ICH Q10 | Same underlying batch data; EU GMP requires additional electronic signature/audit trail per Annex 11 |
| Computer system validation | 21 CFR Part 211 §211.68 + Part 11, EU GMP Annex 11, GAMP 5 | All three reference GAMP 5 methodology; validation records shared |
| CAPA | 21 CFR Part 211 §211.192 (discrepancy investigation), ICH Q10 CAPA, ISO 13485 §8.5.2 | Different triggers; CAPA record structure is harmonized across all three by most quality systems |
| Stability testing | 21 CFR Part 211 §211.166, ICH Q1A–Q1F guidelines | ICH Q1A provides the stability study design guidance that §211.166 is assessed against |
| Complaint handling | 21 CFR Part 211 §211.198, 21 CFR Part 314.81 (post-market reporting), EU MDR (if applicable) | Same complaint file; different reporting triggers for MDR vs. pharmaceutical adverse events |
