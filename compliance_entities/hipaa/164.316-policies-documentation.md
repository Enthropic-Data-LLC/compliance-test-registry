# §164.316 — Policies, Procedures, and Documentation

**Registry path:** `/regulation-registry/HIPAA/164.316/`
**Version:** Current (45 CFR Part 164 Subpart C)
**Last parsed:** 2026-05-20
**Applies to:** Covered entities (healthcare providers who transmit health information electronically, health plans, healthcare clearinghouses) and their business associates who create, receive, maintain, or transmit PHI on behalf of a covered entity
**Trigger:** Creation, receipt, maintenance, or transmission of Protected Health Information (PHI) in connection with a covered function; business associate agreements (BAAs) flow down requirements to subcontractors
**Jurisdiction:** United States; extraterritorial reach for foreign entities handling PHI of US patients or providing services to US covered entities
**Not applicable to:** Life insurers outside healthcare coverage; employers' employment records; education records covered by FERPA; de-identified data meeting Safe Harbor or Expert Determination standard; purely personal health records with no commercial healthcare function
**Overall confidence:** HIGH for 6-year retention (DETERMINISTIC); MEDIUM for policy content and update cadence (PARAMETERIZED)
**R = Required**

---

## Scope summary

§164.316 is the documentation backbone of the HIPAA Security Rule. It requires that all security decisions be in writing, that records be retained for six years, and that policies be updated when the environment changes. It applies to every other Security Rule section — every standard and implementation specification must be supported by written policies, procedures, or other documentation.

The six-year retention period is one of HIPAA's few bright-line thresholds and is fully DETERMINISTIC. The policy content and update cadence requirements are PARAMETERIZED because the rule does not define what "reasonable and appropriate" policies look like.

**Assumption (ASSUME-316-002):** "Periodic" review is satisfied by annual review of all Security Rule policies. Triggered reviews are required when: new ePHI system deployed, significant change to existing system, workforce change affecting security roles, breach or security incident, change in regulatory requirements, or third-party audit finding. ### `tests/164.316/test_b2_retention.py`

```python
"""
164.316(b)(2)(i) — 6-Year Documentation Retention
Confidence: HIGH | Human Review: NOT REQUIRED
"""
from datetime import date
from dateutil.relativedelta import relativedelta  # pip install python-dateutil

RETENTION_YEARS = 6


def test_documentation_retained_for_6_years(policy_inventory):
    today = date.today()
    violations = []
    for policy in policy_inventory:
        creation = policy.get("creation_date")
        last_effective = policy.get("last_effective_date")
        if not creation:
            violations.append(
                f"Policy {policy.get('standard_id', '?')}: no creation date recorded"
            )
            continue
        start_of_retention = max(
            creation,
            last_effective if last_effective else creation,
        )
        retention_end = start_of_retention + relativedelta(years=RETENTION_YEARS)
        if policy.get("purge_date") and policy["purge_date"] < retention_end:
            violations.append(
                f"Policy {policy['standard_id']}: purge date {policy['purge_date']} "
                f"is before required retention end date {retention_end}"
            )
    assert not violations, (
        f"VIOLATION: {len(violations)} policy/policies scheduled for premature purge:\n"
        + "\n".join(violations)
    )


def test_documentation_available_to_responsible_parties(policy_inventory):
    violations = [
        p for p in policy_inventory
        if p.get("active")
        and not p.get("accessible_to_responsible_parties")
    ]
    assert not violations, (
        f"VIOLATION: {len(violations)} active policy/policies not accessible to "
        f"responsible implementing parties: "
        f"{[p['standard_id'] for p in violations]}"
    )
```

```python
import pytest
from datetime import datetime, timedelta

ANNUAL_REVIEW_INTERVAL_DAYS = 365


@pytest.mark.assumption(

    for policy in policy_inventory:
        if not policy.get("active"):

        if not last_reviewed:

        f"VIOLATION: {len(violations)} policy/policies overdue for annual review:\n"
```

## Notes for the registry

- **Retention clock reset on effective date:** If a policy is superseded by a newer version, the 6-year clock for the old version starts from the date the *old version was last in effect* (i.e., the day before the new version took effect), not the old version's creation date. Keep both dates in the `policy_inventory` schema.
- **Retain superseded versions:** Superseded policies must be retained for 6 years from their last-effective date. Do not delete old versions when a policy is updated — archive them with their effective date range clearly documented.
- **Audit trail for policy changes:** Every policy update should create an immutable record: who changed it, what changed, and why. This satisfies both §164.316 documentation requirements and supports OCR audit response.
- **Template-based policies are a common OCR finding:** Organizations that use HIPAA policy templates (from trade associations, law firms, or compliance vendors) without customization are regularly cited for policies that "do not reflect actual practices." The adequacy assumption explicitly requires organization-specific content.
- **Electronic form is acceptable:** The regulation explicitly allows policies in electronic form. PDF, document management system, SharePoint, or similar electronic storage satisfies the written requirement — but must include access controls and be accessible to responsible parties on their schedule.
