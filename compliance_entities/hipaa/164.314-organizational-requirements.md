## Scope summary

§164.314 requires covered entities to have Business Associate Agreements (BAAs) with every business associate before disclosing ePHI. Since HITECH (2009), business associates are directly liable for HIPAA violations regardless of whether a BAA is in place — but the absence of a BAA is itself a separate violation. BAAs are the most frequently cited deficiency in OCR enforcement actions.

A business associate is any person or entity that performs functions or activities on behalf of a covered entity involving the use or disclosure of PHI. This includes: cloud storage providers, billing services, EHR vendors, IT managed service providers, lawyers handling patient information, accountants reviewing billing records, consultants with ePHI access, and subcontractors of BAs.

## (a)(1) — Business Associate Contracts (R — DETERMINISTIC for existence)

### Source excerpt

> *A covered entity may permit a business associate to create, receive, maintain, or transmit electronic protected health information on the covered entity's behalf only if the covered entity obtains satisfactory assurances, in accordance with § 164.314(a)(2), that the business associate will appropriately safeguard the information.*

> *A covered entity is not in compliance with the standards in § 164.314(a)(1) if the covered entity knew of a pattern or practice of activity by the business associate that constituted a material breach or violation of the business associate's obligation and failed to take reasonable steps to cure the breach or end the violation.*

## (a)(2) — Required BAA Content (R — PARAMETERIZED)

### Source excerpt (abbreviated — full list at §164.314(a)(2)(i))

> The contract or other arrangement must provide that the business associate will:
> - Not use or disclose PHI other than as permitted or required by the contract or required by law
> - Use appropriate safeguards and comply with Subpart C (Security Rule) for ePHI
> - Report to the covered entity any security incident of which it becomes aware, including breaches
> - Ensure that subcontractors agree to the same restrictions and conditions that apply to the BA
> - Make PHI available as required for individual rights (access, amendment, accounting)
> - Make its internal practices available to HHS for compliance audits
> - Return or destroy PHI upon termination of contract
> - Authorize termination of contract if CE discovers material violation

**Assumption (ASSUME-314-001):** A BAA is content-complete when it includes: (1) permitted use/disclosure scope; (2) Security Rule compliance for ePHI; (3) security incident and breach reporting obligation (with a defined reporting timeframe — 60 days is the maximum but a shorter period is strongly recommended); (4) subcontractor flow-down; (5) individual rights support (access, amendment, accounting of disclosures); (6) HHS audit access; (7) return or destruction upon termination; (8) CE right to terminate for material violation.

## BAA lifecycle management

### Termination and offboarding

| Event | Obligation | Classification |
|---|---|---|
| Contract ends | Return or destroy ePHI; obtain written confirmation | DETERMINISTIC |
| Material violation discovered | CE must take reasonable steps to cure or terminate within reasonable time | PARAMETERIZED |
| BA acquired/merged | BAA with acquiring entity required before ePHI transfer continues | DETERMINISTIC |

### Subcontractor flow-down

Since HITECH, business associates must require their subcontractors to agree to the same restrictions as the BA. Subcontractor BAAs must flow down to any entity that handles ePHI on the BA's behalf. The covered entity does not hold BAAs with sub-BAs directly — that is the BA's obligation — but the CE's BAA with the BA must require this flow-down.

"""
164.314(a)(1) — Business Associate Agreement Existence
Confidence: HIGH | Human Review: NOT REQUIRED
"""
from datetime import datetime


def test_all_ephi_vendors_have_active_baa(ephi_vendors, baa_records):
    baa_vendor_ids = {
        r["vendor_id"] for r in baa_records if r.get("status") == "active"
    }
    missing = [
        v for v in ephi_vendors
        if v["vendor_id"] not in baa_vendor_ids
    ]
    assert not missing, (
        f"CRITICAL VIOLATION: {len(missing)} vendor(s) with ePHI access have no "
        f"active BAA (§164.314(a)(1)): "
        f"{[(v['vendor_id'], v.get('vendor_name')) for v in missing]}"
    )


def test_baa_signed_before_first_ephi_disclosure(ephi_vendors, baa_records):
    violations = []
    for vendor in ephi_vendors:
        baa = next(
            (r for r in baa_records if r["vendor_id"] == vendor["vendor_id"]),
            None,
        )
        if not baa:
            continue  # covered by existence test above
        if (
            baa.get("signed_date")
            and vendor.get("first_ephi_disclosure_date")
            and baa["signed_date"] > vendor["first_ephi_disclosure_date"]
        ):
            violations.append(
                f"Vendor {vendor['vendor_id']}: BAA signed {baa['signed_date']} "
                f"AFTER first ePHI disclosure {vendor['first_ephi_disclosure_date']}"
            )
    assert not violations, (
        f"VIOLATION: {len(violations)} BAA(s) signed after ePHI was already "
        f"disclosed:\n" + "\n".join(violations)
    )


def test_baa_signed_by_both_parties(baa_records):
    unsigned = [
        r for r in baa_records
        if r.get("status") == "active"
        and not r.get("signed_by_both_parties")
    ]
    assert not unsigned, (
        f"VIOLATION: {len(unsigned)} active BAA(s) not signed by both parties: "
        f"{[r['vendor_id'] for r in unsigned]}"
    )


def test_no_expired_baas_with_active_ephi_relationship(ephi_vendors, baa_records):
    violations = []
    today = datetime.utcnow().date()
    for vendor in ephi_vendors:
        active_baa = next(
            (r for r in baa_records
             if r["vendor_id"] == vendor["vendor_id"]
             and r.get("status") == "active"),
            None,
        )
        if not active_baa:
            continue
        if active_baa.get("expiry_date") and active_baa["expiry_date"] < today:
            violations.append(
                f"Vendor {vendor['vendor_id']}: BAA expired {active_baa['expiry_date']} "
                f"but relationship still active"
            )
    assert not violations, (
        f"VIOLATION: {len(violations)} vendor(s) with expired BAA and ongoing "
        f"ePHI access:\n" + "\n".join(violations)
    )

"""
164.314(a)(2) — BAA Required Content Clauses
Confidence: MEDIUM | Human Review: ASSUMPTION REQUIRED (ASSUME-314-001)
"""
import pytest

REQUIRED_BAA_CLAUSES = {
    "permitted_use_disclosure_scope",
    "security_rule_compliance",
    "incident_breach_reporting",
    "subcontractor_flow_down",
    "individual_rights_support",
    "hhs_audit_access",
    "return_or_destroy_on_termination",
    "ce_right_to_terminate_for_violation",
}


@pytest.mark.assumption(
    id="ASSUME-314-001",
    description=(
        "BAA content-complete: all 8 required clauses present; "
        "breach reporting timeframe ≤ 60 days (30 recommended); "
        "return/destroy clause specifies method and written confirmation"
    ),
    approved_by="Compliance Officer",
    review_date="2026-05-20",
)
def test_all_baas_have_required_clauses(baa_records, baa_content_reviews):
    violations = []
    review_map = {r["vendor_id"]: r for r in baa_content_reviews}
    for baa in baa_records:
        if baa.get("status") != "active":
            continue
        review = review_map.get(baa["vendor_id"])
        if not review:
            violations.append(
                f"Vendor {baa['vendor_id']}: no BAA content review on file"
            )
            continue
        present = set(review.get("required_clauses_present", []))
        missing = REQUIRED_BAA_CLAUSES - present
        if missing:
            violations.append(
                f"Vendor {baa['vendor_id']}: BAA missing clauses {missing}"
            )
    assert not violations, (
        f"VIOLATION: {len(violations)} BAA(s) with missing required content:\n"
        + "\n".join(violations)
    )


@pytest.mark.assumption(id="ASSUME-314-001")
def test_baa_breach_reporting_timeframe_acceptable(baa_records, baa_content_reviews):
    MAX_BREACH_REPORTING_DAYS = 60
    violations = []
    review_map = {r["vendor_id"]: r for r in baa_content_reviews}
    for baa in baa_records:
        if baa.get("status") != "active":
            continue
        review = review_map.get(baa["vendor_id"])
        if not review:
            continue
        reporting_days = review.get("breach_reporting_days")
        if reporting_days and reporting_days > MAX_BREACH_REPORTING_DAYS:
            violations.append(
                f"Vendor {baa['vendor_id']}: breach reporting timeframe "
                f"{reporting_days} days exceeds {MAX_BREACH_REPORTING_DAYS}-day maximum"
            )
    assert not violations, (
        f"VIOLATION: {len(violations)} BAA(s) with excessive breach reporting "
        f"timeframe:\n" + "\n".join(violations)
    )


def test_baa_vendor_inventory_matches_ephi_vendor_list(ephi_vendors, baa_records):
    """Cross-check: every vendor in the BAA registry should be in the ePHI vendor inventory."""
    ephi_vendor_ids = {v["vendor_id"] for v in ephi_vendors}

        r for r in baa_records
        if r.get("status") == "active"
        and r["vendor_id"] not in ephi_vendor_ids
    ]
    if orphan_baas:
        # Non-blocking: BAA without matching vendor record could be a data entry gap

            f"ePHI vendor inventory — verify these relationships are still active: "
            f"{[r['vendor_id'] for r in orphan_baas]}"
        )

## Notes for the registry

- **OCR most-cited violation:** Missing or expired BAAs are consistently the top or second-top citation in OCR enforcement. Most Resolution Agreements include a BAA remediation requirement. This is the single highest-value test in the HIPAA registry.
- **Cloud providers:** Major cloud providers (AWS, Azure, GCP, Microsoft 365) all offer BAAs (called "HIPAA Business Associate Addendums" or "Data Processing Addendums"). Verify the BAA is actually signed/accepted and covers the specific services in use — general BAAs do not cover services that are not HIPAA-eligible.
- **De-identified data exception:** A vendor that only receives de-identified data (per 45 CFR §164.514) is not a business associate and does not require a BAA. However, the de-identification must be performed per Safe Harbor or Expert Determination method and documented. Do not rely on this exception without formal de-identification documentation.
- **Conduit exception:** Entities that merely transport ePHI without accessing it (e.g., plain email carriers, postal services, internet service providers) are not business associates. However, vendors that store ePHI even temporarily (e.g., cloud email with archiving, backup providers) are BAs.
- **Group health plan special rule:** §164.314(b) applies to group health plans — it requires plan documents to be amended to permit disclosure of ePHI to the plan sponsor only for specific purposes. This is an additional organizational requirement for employer-sponsored health plans.
